# Back to School Checklist 2026: For Students, Teachers & Parents

The school year starts in August. The people who feel prepared in August started in July.

This isn't about buying new supplies (though that's on the list too). It's about setting up systems that will carry you through the year without breaking down in October when everything gets hard.

Separate checklists below for the three roles that are trying to get ready at the same time.

---

## For Students: High School & College

### Before school starts

- [ ] Get your class schedule (high school) or course registrations confirmed (college)
- [ ] Map out your classes physically — which buildings, which floors, how far between them
- [ ] Set up a dedicated study space at home (or identify where you'll work at school)
- [ ] Buy or download a planner for the academic year (not the same as a calendar — a planner has space for assignments by class)
- [ ] Set up your email with a filter for school communications so they don't get buried
- [ ] Get any required texts — check the library and used-textbook sites before buying new
- [ ] Update your backpack situation — broken zippers and missing straps become problems in week two

### Supplies that actually matter

- [ ] A way to take notes (laptop, iPad + Apple Pencil, or paper notebooks — pick the system and commit)
- [ ] If paper: separate notebooks per class OR a large binder with dividers (mixing these causes chaos)
- [ ] Highlighters in 3-4 colors (one per class or one per priority level)
- [ ] Earbuds or headphones that work for studying
- [ ] Phone charger with a long cable (backpack + desk)
- [ ] A reusable water bottle you'll actually carry

### Planning setup

- [ ] Write every known deadline into your planner before week one (syllabi have them all)
- [ ] Set up a weekly review time — Sunday evening works for most students
- [ ] Figure out your exam-prep approach for the first round before it arrives (not during)
- [ ] For college students: identify office hours for every professor before you need them

## For Teachers: Back-to-School Prep

### Before students arrive

- [ ] Class roster finalized with correct spellings (you'll say every name in front of them)
- [ ] Seating chart planned or your system for the first day ready
- [ ] Emergency sub packet prepared and filed in the right place
- [ ] Parent communication plan decided: What will you use (email, app, newsletters)? How often?
- [ ] Gradebook or grade tracking system set up
- [ ] Lesson plans for weeks 1-2 drafted (not just "intro week" vibes — actual plans)
- [ ] Supply inventory done and restocking orders submitted before the rush
- [ ] School policies reviewed — things change year to year

### Classroom setup

- [ ] Bulletin board and wall displays ready (or at least planned)
- [ ] Student materials organized so distribution takes under 5 minutes
- [ ] Your desk organized enough to find things under pressure on Day 1
- [ ] Procedures for the first week explicitly planned: bathroom, pencils, late work, all of it

### Year-long planning

- [ ] Academic calendar marked with school-wide events (PD days, breaks, testing windows)
- [ ] Major unit milestones mapped so you know when you're behind before you're behind
- [ ] Parent contact log started (even if empty — the structure matters)
- [ ] Professional development goals set before admin sets them for you

## For Parents: Supporting School-Age Kids

### Before school starts

- [ ] Back-to-school physical / dental appointment scheduled (if required or overdue)
- [ ] School supply list obtained and purchased without buying the wrong size or color
- [ ] Backpack and lunch bag checked — does it still fit? Does the zipper work?
- [ ] Morning routine planned and explained to kids before Day 1 (not discovered on Day 1)
- [ ] After-school care, pickup arrangements, and activity schedule confirmed
- [ ] Emergency contacts and pickup authorization at the school office updated
- [ ] School calendar added to your family calendar

### Family planning

- [ ] Homework time block added to the family schedule (not "whenever it gets done")
- [ ] Dinner plan adjusted if you're picking up later during the week
- [ ] Activity and sports schedule for fall confirmed and put somewhere everyone can see it
- [ ] Communication preferences set with teachers: email or app, and when you'll actually check it
- [ ] Budget for the school year set (activities, supplies, events, class photos)

## When to Start

The people who feel most prepared in August started setting up their systems in July.

If you're reading this in July: you're ahead of schedule. Start now.
