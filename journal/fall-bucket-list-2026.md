# Fall Bucket List 2026: 50 Things to Do Before December

Autumn is the season most people mean to make the most of and never quite do.

September arrives with the best of intentions. Pumpkin patches, apple orchards, cozy Sunday soups. Then October shows up and you realize you've been inside the entire time and the leaves are already gone. November is a blur of work deadlines and Thanksgiving logistics.

If you want to actually *do* autumn instead of just surviving it, you need a list before it starts — not after it's over.

Here's 50 ideas to make fall 2026 your best one yet.

---

## 🍎 Food & Drink (Things That Taste Like Fall)

1. Make a big pot of homemade chili from scratch
2. Visit an apple orchard and pick your own apples
3. Bake an apple pie or apple crisp
4. Try a pumpkin spice recipe you've never made before
5. Do a hot apple cider tasting (regular, spiced, sparkling)
6. Make a fall charcuterie board with apples, pears, gouda, and pecans
7. Cook a slow-cooker soup on a Sunday and eat it all week
8. Try a new fall cocktail or mocktail (apple mule, bourbon peach, spiced cranberry)
9. Visit a farmer's market while they're still open
10. Make caramel apples at home

## How to Actually Do the List

Most bucket lists fail for the same reason: they live in your head or in a notes app you open once and forget.

The ones that work are the ones that get tracked. You need to be able to look at the list mid-October and know what you've done and what's slipping.

A few things that help:

**Print it out.** Something physical that you can check off works better than a digital list for this kind of goal. The act of checking a box is small but it's real.

**Put it somewhere you'll see it.** On the fridge, at your desk, inside your planner. Not in a folder.

**Schedule at least three items into your actual calendar.** "I'll do it sometime in October" doesn't work. "Saturday October 11th, apple orchard, 10am" does.

**Review it weekly.** Takes two minutes. What's done? What's getting scheduled next?
