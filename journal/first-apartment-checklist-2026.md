Moving into your first apartment is a huge milestone — and a surprisingly long to-do list. Between figuring out utilities, furnishing every room, and remembering every little thing you never noticed your parents bought, it's easy to feel overwhelmed.

This first apartment checklist covers everything you actually need, room by room, plus the adulting essentials nobody warns you about. Whether you're moving in solo, with a roommate, or with a partner, use this as your master guide.

---

## The Big Picture First: Before You Move In

Before you touch a box, knock out these fundamentals.

### ✅ Paperwork & Utilities

- Sign lease and keep a copy in a safe place
- Pay first month's rent and security deposit (get receipts)
- Set up electricity (call or go online 1–2 weeks early)
- Set up gas if applicable
- Set up internet — order 2 weeks out, installation slots fill fast
- Set up renters insurance (often under $20/month — get it before move-in day)
- Update your address: USPS, bank, employer, subscriptions, driver's license
- Get keys, mailbox key, parking permit, and gate codes
- Do a walk-through with your landlord and document any existing damage (photos + written notice)

### ✅ Moving Day Logistics

- Reserve elevator or loading dock if in an apartment building
- Book movers or rent a truck (book 2–3 weeks early in summer)
- Get sturdy boxes, packing tape, markers, and bubble wrap
- Pack an "open first" box: toilet paper, towels, phone charger, snacks, coffee maker, shower curtain

## Kitchen Essentials

The kitchen will eat your budget if you're not careful. Here's what you actually need vs. what can wait.

### Must-Have Kitchen Items

**Cookware**
- 10" or 12" non-stick skillet
- 2–3 qt saucepan with lid
- Large pot (for pasta, soups)
- Baking sheet

**Utensils & Tools**
- Chef's knife + cutting board
- Wooden spoon, spatula, ladle
- Measuring cups and spoons
- Can opener
- Vegetable peeler
- Colander/strainer
- Mixing bowls (a set of 3)

**Appliances**
- Coffee maker or electric kettle
- Toaster
- Microwave (check if included)
- Blender (optional but high-use)

**Dishes & Storage**
- Plates, bowls, mugs (4 of each is enough to start)
- Glasses + wine glasses
- Silverware set
- Food storage containers (airtight — prevents pests)
- Dish drying rack
- Dish soap, sponge, trash bags
- Paper towels + holder
- Aluminum foil, plastic wrap, parchment paper

**Pantry Starters**
- Olive oil, salt, pepper
- Pasta, rice, canned tomatoes
- Coffee, tea
- Spices: garlic powder, onion powder, Italian seasoning
- Condiments: hot sauce, soy sauce, your usuals

## Bedroom Essentials

### Must-Have Bedroom Items

- Bed frame + mattress (measure your space before buying — a queen barely fits in a small bedroom)
- Box spring or platform (check what your mattress needs)
- Pillow (1–2)
- Sheets — at least 2 sets so you can wash one and still sleep
- Duvet or comforter + duvet cover
- Mattress protector (waterproof — you will thank yourself)
- Nightstand or bedside table
- Lamp (natural light is rare in many apartments)
- Dresser or clothing storage
- Hangers (buy more than you think you need)
- Full-length mirror (incredibly useful)
- Blackout curtains (game changer for sleep quality)
- Laundry hamper

## Bathroom Essentials

### Must-Have Bathroom Items

- Shower curtain + liner + curtain rings (most apartments don't come with these)
- Bath towels (2–3 sets)
- Hand towels + washcloths
- Bath mat
- Toilet brush + plunger (yes, get the plunger before you need it)
- Toilet paper (buy a bulk pack on day 1)
- Soap dispenser + hand soap
- Shampoo, conditioner, body wash
- Toothbrush holder
- Hair dryer
- First aid kit: bandages, pain reliever, thermometer, antiseptic

### Medicine Cabinet Must-Haves

- Pain relievers (ibuprofen, acetaminophen)
- Allergy medicine
- Cold medicine
- Antacids
- Bandages in multiple sizes
- Antibiotic ointment
- Tweezers

## Adulting Essentials Nobody Tells You About

These are the things first-time apartment dwellers routinely forget — and feel the pain of at 11 PM on a Sunday.

### 🔧 Tools (You Need These)

- Hammer
- Screwdrivers (flathead + Phillips)
- Drill or electric screwdriver
- Level
- Measuring tape
- Allen wrench set (for IKEA furniture)
- Pliers
- Flashlight or headlamp

### 💡 Random Essentials

- Surge protectors (protect your electronics)
- Batteries (AA, AAA — you'll always need them)
- Light bulbs (apartments sometimes have none)
- Command hooks and strips (hang things without losing your security deposit)
- Doorstop
- Carbon monoxide detector + smoke detector (check if included; if not, buy one)
- Fire extinguisher (small kitchen one)
- Duct tape
- WD-40

### 📋 Important Documents to Keep on File

- Copy of lease agreement
- Renters insurance policy
- Utility account numbers
- Emergency contact list for building/landlord
- Move-in inspection report with photos

## Budget: What Does a First Apartment Actually Cost to Set Up?

Here's a realistic breakdown for setting up a 1-bedroom apartment from scratch:

| Category | Budget Option | Mid-Range |

| Bed + bedding | $400–$600 | $800–$1,200 |
| Kitchen setup | $150–$300 | $400–$700 |
| Bathroom | $100–$150 | $200–$300 |
| Cleaning supplies | $75–$100 | $100–$150 |
| Living room basics | $500–$800 | $1,200–$2,500 |
| Miscellaneous tools | $100–$150 | $200–$300 |
| **Total** | **~$1,300–$2,100** | **~$2,900–$5,150** |

Tips to cut costs:
- Facebook Marketplace and Craigslist for furniture (sofas, dressers, bed frames)
- IKEA for kitchen and storage basics
- Dollar stores for cleaning supplies
- Buy generic bedding from Target or Amazon — thread count matters less than you think

## Moving Timeline: Week-by-Week Plan

### 6 Weeks Out
- Sign lease, pay deposits
- Start collecting boxes (liquor stores often give them free)
- Begin purging stuff you won't bring

### 4 Weeks Out
- Book movers or truck
- Set up utilities (electricity, gas, internet)
- Get renters insurance
- Order or purchase large furniture items with long lead times

### 2 Weeks Out
- Start packing non-essential items
- Update your address (USPS, bank, work)
- Deep-clean your current place as you pack

### Moving Week
- Pack room by room, label every box clearly (room + contents)
- Pack "open first" box last so it's first off the truck
- Confirm movers/truck, elevator reservation, parking

### Move-In Day
- Do walk-through and document existing damage
- Get keys, parking passes, mailbox key
- Set up bathroom + bedroom first — you need to sleep and shower tonight
- Kitchen and everything else can wait until day 2

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What should I buy first for my first apartment?**
Start with the basics you'll need the first night: toilet paper, shower curtain and liner, towels, bedding, and a few kitchen items (coffee maker, dishes, basic cookware). Set up your bathroom and bedroom first — you can live without the rest for a few days.

**How much money should I save before moving into my first apartment?**
Aim for 3–4 months of rent saved before moving. You'll need first month, last month, and security deposit up front — that's often 2–3 months' rent before you've paid for a single piece of furniture. Budget an additional $1,500–$2,500 for setup costs.

**What do most people forget when moving into their first apartment?**
Common forgotten items: shower curtain + liner, toilet plunger, cleaning supplies, light bulbs, trash cans, hangers, and basic tools. These seem obvious until 10 PM on move-in night when you realize you have none of them.

**Should I buy furniture before or after moving in?**
Measure your rooms first, then buy. Sofas and bed frames that seem normal-sized at the store can overwhelm a small apartment bedroom. Buy a bed and basic kitchen gear before move-in. Everything else can come over the first few weeks.

**Do I need renters insurance?**
Yes. It's typically $15–$25/month and covers your personal belongings if there's a fire, theft, or water damage. Some landlords require it. It's one of the most cost-effective things you can do.
